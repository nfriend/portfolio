PSI Dashboard is a simple project visualizer that increases project transparency, aids in portfolio planning, and helps identify cross-team dependencies.


Dates:
May - December, 2014
Technologies:
AngularJS, ASP.NET Web Api 2
Project type:
Work project

Purpose

Tools like TFS provide large corporations with a seamless way to organize, plan, and execute and their software development efforts. Unfortunately, most project management tools focus on individual teams and team workflows, and neglect to include a simple way to aggregate all of a company's development metrics into a single view. PSI Dashboard aims to fill this gap by providing a glanceable frontend that communicates the organization's developement progress as a whole.

The PSI Concept

DuPont Pioneer organizes its development cadence into eight week (four sprint) chunks. These chunks are known as Potentially Shippable Increments, or PSIs. At the end of every PSI boundary, each development team is expected to have a releasable product that includes the features that have been under development for the past two months.

While the concept of a regular release schedule is hardly unique to Pioneer, the specific implementation and nomenclature of this process made working with third-party management tools awkward at best.
